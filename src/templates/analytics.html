Biological Timeline Integration

The model implicitly captures the complete dengue transmission cycle through lag features that span the entire biological timeline:

Days 0-14: Mosquito Development
Aedes aegypti mosquitoes develop from eggs to adults in 10-14 days under optimal conditions (warm temperatures, standing water).
Days 0-12: Virus Incubation (Mosquito)
After biting an infected person, the virus incubates in the mosquito for 8-12 days before it becomes infectious.
Days 0-7: Human Incubation
Once a person is bitten by an infected mosquito, symptoms appear after 4-7 days of incubation.
3-Week Lag Features
The model's lag features (Cases_last_week, Cases_2_weeks_ago, Cases_3week_avg) effectively capture this ~3-week transmission cycle, allowing the system to learn outbreak momentum patterns without explicit biological modeling.
